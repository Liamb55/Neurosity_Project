Kinesis

The main objective of brain computer interfacing (BCI) is to replace or restore useful function to people disabled by neuromuscular disorders.

In the regard, the Crown has two main advantages: - Users have access to their own collected data which is not the case with other headsets where you have to pay to access your own data. - the Crown also provides a function called kinesis. Kinesis is responses to movement. In this case, the Crown captures eeg signals in response to imagined movement.

If the kinesis signals associated with a certain imagined action can be captured and then replicated, the signal can be used as a switch to for example turn on lights, television etc which would be of great benefits to people paralyzed.

This is very possible to achieve as is demonstrated in the attached video where AJ Kellar a founder of Neurosity controls his computer mouse using brain eeg signals.



Theory Behind Kinesis

The theory here is that the motor cortex in the brain sends eeg signals when we imagine movement, for example moving your right index finger. These signals can then be replicated to act as a switch.

The Motor Cortex


The different areas of the motor cortex send eeg signals corresponding to various parts of the body such as fingers, hands, legs feet etc. The points on the crown probably most linked to the motor cortex are nemely F5, PO3, PO4, F6.
